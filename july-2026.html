Monthly Archive

July 2026

A continuing archive of words, meanings, and sentences gathered through July.

July 1

New Word: Peripatetic = (Adj.) Constantly moving rather than staying in one location.

New Sentence: My own life has been more peripatetic than theirs.

July 2

New Word: Precocity = (N.) exceptional intellectual, artistic, or emotional development in a child or young person.

New Sentence: The novel explores the burden of childhood precocity.

July 3

New Word: Limerick = (N.) a short, humorous poem of five lines with a distinctive rhyme and rhythm.

New Sentence: Perhaps as a result of reading Edward Lear, I became a fan of the limerick form.

July 6

New Word: Garret = (N.) a small room or apartment just below the roof of a house, typically in the attic.

New Sentence: It was the perfect author's garret.

July 7

New Word: Onanistic = (Adj.) relating to masturbation or self-gratification.

New Sentence: These self-glorifying prose fantasies offered a fleeting, onanistic comfort, usually followed by a pang of shame.

June 8

New Word: Plod = (V.) to walk or work slowly, steadily, and with effort.

New Sentence: After the slow plod of its first act—and particularly thanks to Law—the movie does arrive at something mesmerizing.

June 9

New Word: Taciturn = (Adj.) habitually quiet or reserved.

New Sentence: The normally taciturn coach smiled after the victory.

June 10

New Word: Jettisoned = (V.) Figuratively (much more common today): to discard, abandon, or get rid of something deliberately.

New Sentence: A relatively mild recession, fueled by higher oil prices brought on by the Persian Gulf war, had jettisoned roughly 1.5 million jobs from the U.S. economy and intensified competition for entry-level positions.

June 11

New Word: Floundering = (V.) to struggle helplessly or make little progress because of confusion, difficulty, or lack of direction.

New Sentence: Rather than floundering, they discovered fulfilling, if occasionally unexpected, careers.

June 12

New Word: Gerrymandering = (N.) the deliberate manipulation of electoral district boundaries to give one political party or group an unfair advantage in elections.

New Sentence: Canadian gerrymandering is at the expense of urban voters.

June 15

New Word: Spidey-sense = (N.) an intuitive feeling that something is about to happen or that something isn't quite right.

New Sentence: My Spidey-sense tells me something is off.

June 16

New Word: Chuffed = (Adj.) Very pleased or delighted.

New Sentence: I'm chuffed with the result.

June 17

New Word: Yanked = (V.) to remove, drag, or jerk something with a quick, sharp motion.

New Sentence: Alcohol as a lever in trade talks gained an even higher profile when provinces yanked U.S.-made bottles from store shelves in response to President Donald Trump’s tariffs and threats against Canadian sovereignty.

June 18

New Word: Catnip = (N.) Something irressitible to a particular group, appealing almost to an irrational degree.

New Sentence: “The scandal was catnip for cable news.”

June 19

New Word: Skulk = (V.) to avoid notice, often because of guilt, fear, or suspicious intent.

New Sentence: Photographers were accused of skulking outside the celebrity’s home.

June 22

New Word: Propensity = (N.) A habit or trait that makes someone likely to do something.

New Sentence: Messi’s propensity to lurk, rather than harry, adds to his threat.

June 23

New Word: Impinge = (V.) to encroach upon or infringe upon someone else's rights, freedom, time, or space.

New Sentence: Public events impinge so directly on our private lives.

June 24

New Word: Demagogue = (N.) a political leader who gains power by appealing to people's emotions, fears, or prejudices rather than by using reasoned arguments or sound policies.

New Sentence: A celebrity president who is a populist demagogue.

June 25

New Word: massif = (N.) A principal mountain mass bounded by fault lines which move as one unit.

New Sentence: The details are doing more work here than the headline.

June 26

New Word: Disingenuous = (Adj.) Lacking in frankness, candour; insincere

New Sentence: Don't bury the lead.

June 29

New Word: Balmy = (Adj.) Pleasantly warm and mild.

New Sentence: Reading should be subject not to our plans but to our whims.

June 30

New Word: Irreverently = (Adv.) In a cheeky and plafully disrespectful way, not in a hostile way.

New Sentence: Some one who comes from outside has no idea where the bodies are buried.
